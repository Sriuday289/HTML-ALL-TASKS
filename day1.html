TYPES OF HEADING TAGS
HTML provides six levels of heading tags (<h1gt;to <h6>), each used to define headings in a document. These tags are essential for structuring content and improving readability. Below is an overview of each heading tag: 1. <h1> (Main Heading) Represents the most important heading on the page. Typically used for the main title or the primary focus of the content. Example: <h1>Welcome to Menaka Novelties</h1> 2. <h2> (Subheading) Used for subheadings under the main heading. Helps organize sections within the page. Example: <h2>About Us</h2> 3. <h3> (Sub-Subheading) Suitable for further subsections within an <h2gt; section. Example: <h3gt;Our Product Categories</h3> 4. <h4> (Sub-Subheading Level 2) Used for deeper divisions under <h3>. Example: <h4>Cosmetics</h4> 5. <h5> (Sub-Subheading Level 3) Represents even more specific content under <h4>. Example: <h6>Terms and Conditions <h6gt; Example: <h5>Popular Brands </h5> Example: <h6>Terms and Conditions </h6> 6. <h6gt; (Lowest Heading Level) The least prominent heading, used for minor details. Example: <h6>Terms and Conditions <h6> Example: <h6>Terms and Conditions <h6> Best Practices: Hierarchy: Always use headings in a logical order (<h1> to <h6>). Do not skip levels. SEO Importance: Proper use of headings improves search engine optimization (SEO) and accessibility. Readable Structure: Headings break content into manageable sections, making it easier for users to navigate the page.
